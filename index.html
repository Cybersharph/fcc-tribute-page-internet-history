Tim Berners-Lee

The Founder of the World Wide Web

Here's a timeline of Tim Berners-Lee's life:

  • 1955 - Born in London, England.
  • 1980 - Proposes the concept of hypertext while working at CERN.
  • 1989 - Invents the World Wide Web, allowing for the sharing of information over the internet.
  • 1990 - Develops the first web browser, editor, and the first website.
  • 1994 - Establishes the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at MIT to oversee web standards.
  • 2004 - Knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his pioneering work on the internet.
  • 2009 - Receives the prestigious Order of Merit from Queen Elizabeth II.
  • 2012 - Becomes a vocal advocate for net neutrality and an open internet.
  • 2018 - Launches the "Contract for the Web," aiming to protect online rights and privacy.
  • 2020 - Continues to work on projects promoting web privacy and user control, including the Solid project.
"The Web as I envisaged it, we have not seen it yet. The future is still so much bigger than the past."
-- Tim Berners-Lee
